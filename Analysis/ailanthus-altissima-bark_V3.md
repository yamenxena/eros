# Geometric & Algorithmic Heuristics of Ailanthus altissima — V3 (Macro/Micro Subdivisions)

## Objective
To evolve the mechanical Ailanthus model into a dual-layered topological system. The tree bark is no longer a single flat voronoi mesh; it is composed of **Deformed Vertical Stripes** (the macroscopic dead suberin plates) that house **Internal Cellular Networks** (the microscopic living parenchyma). These internal cells exhibit *inverse growth*—expanding inward to heal the voids created when the macroscopic stripes are pulled apart by radial tree growth.

## 1. The Macro-Structure: Deformed Vertical Stripes
The primary visual framework consists of massive, continuous vertical columns.
- **Topology**: These stripes run from top to bottom, distorted by SimplexNoise to weave and snake organically.
- **Tension Mechanics**: A lateral tension field pulls these macro-stripes apart along the X-axis (trunk expansion), creating major fissures between them.
- **Spring Net**: The macro-stripes are connected horizontally by structural "snap" springs. Once the tension exceeds the threshold, the macro fissures open up.

## 2. The Micro-Structure: Inverse Cellular Growth
Inside the vertical stripes (and overflowing into the fissures), a secondary biological logic runs:
- **Subdivision**: Each macro-stripe is internally divided into a strict grid of geometrical cells (Quads).
- **Inverse Growth Rule**: While the macro-forces pull the plates *apart*, the internal cells experience a growth force that expands them *inversely* towards the gaps.
- **Non-Overlap Condition**: Using a strict rigid-body repulsion constraint, these internal cells pack together perfectly, filling the structural voids without ever intersecting.
- **The Result**: A highly sophisticated architectural shape. The outer shell splits apart, and geometric cells swell from within to span the gap, naturally creating complex diamond patterns and healing matrices.

## 3. Geometric Rules & Collision
- `Clamp(A ∩ B = ∅)`: A robust topological mesh rule.
- Both the macro-stripes and micro-cells are constrained. The cells act like a constrained spring-mass net. The outer stripe forces the cells, but as the stripe cracks, the cells become liberated to grow outward. 

## 4. Comprehensive Canopy Color Application (Resolved)
The previous palette application was overly segregated (just stroke vs fill). 
In V3, coloring will be applied biologically:
1. **Background Canvas**: Pure Deep Shade (Chestnut / Bark Shadow).
2. **Macro Stripes (Dead Bark)**: Rendered in the Light Gray/Charcoal ranges.
3. **Micro Cells (Living Cambium)**: The cells that grow into the fissures will dynamically map their color based on their *strain energy*. Newly expanding cells will glow with the Yellow-Green and Bronze of the living Ailanthus, flooding the entire canvas with the botanical palette.
