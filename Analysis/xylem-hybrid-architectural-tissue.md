# Xylem: Hybrid Architectural Tissue — Generative Heuristics

## Objective
To synthesize the brutalist spatial partitioning of `Edifice` with the biological, dual-layered inverse growth physics of `Ailanthus`. This creates a completely new generative method, `Xylem`. It envisions a scenario where strict architectural blueprints are violently invaded by swelling, living botanical tissues wherever structural failures (lesions/explosions) occur.

## 1. Macro-Constraint: Orthogonal Partitioning (Edifice)
The canvas is first decomposed via a rigorous procedural composition algorithm (`trunk`, `cross`, `cluster`, `cascade`).
- This determines distinct, rectangular boundary enclosures (`Rects`).
- Each `Rect` acts as an absolute physical clamp. No matter how violent the forces inside, the boundaries remain perfectly orthogonal and architectural.

## 2. Micro-Invasion: The Strain-Swelling Mesh (Ailanthus)
Inside each `Rect`, a highly dense grid of independent `Quads` is initialized.
- **The Lesion Centers**: Randomly distributed "Explosion Nodes" act as violent tensile forces (similar to bomb blasts in Edifice, but acting as rapid traumatic plant growth centers).
- **The Deformation**: The nodes of the Quads are pushed away from the Lesion Centers.
- **The Hybrid Rule (Inverse Callus Swell)**: Instead of the Quads simply ripping into empty space, the `Quads` detect the tensile strain. If a Quad is stretched heavily by an explosion, it triggers its *Inverse Swell Growth* (from Ailanthus). The Quad geometrically expands to heal the torn space, changing its topological area dynamically.

## 3. Kinetic Color Topology
This method utilizes a profound juxtaposition of palettes.
- **Resting Tissue**: Quads unaffected by explosions maintain the strict architectural lines and colors (e.g., Blueprint or Indigo).
- **Infected/Healing Tissue**: As internal strain exceeds a threshold, the Quads violently shift their color mapping into deep Ailanthus Bronze and radiant Yellow-Green.
- **The Result**: A breathtaking visual of strict architectural grids locally infected with organic, colorful growth tumors that bulge into the negative space.
