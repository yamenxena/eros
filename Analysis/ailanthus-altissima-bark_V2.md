# Geometric & Algorithmic Heuristics of Ailanthus altissima — V2 

## Objective
To simulate the diamond-fissured, reticulated growth patterns indicative of mature *Ailanthus altissima* bark, moving beyond simple stochastic particle spread into a rigorous, non-overlapping geometric continuum. The aesthetic goal is a complex, structural grid that deforms under directional growth tension, revealing the living cambium (parenchyma) splitting apart dead, rigid bark plates (suberin).

## 1. Structural Paradigm: Voronoi Crackle and Mechanical Buckling
Real tree bark cracks due to the circumference of the tree increasing. An advanced procedural method to simulate this is mapping a **mass-spring system** over a **Voronoi diagram**. 
- **Substrate Tessellation (Voronoi/Delaunay)**: We begin with a dense, non-overlapping 2D grid of polygonal cells generated by a geometric relaxation algorithm (like Lloyd's algorithm). These polygons represent the dead, rigid plates of bark.
- **The Rule**: These plates are governed by an internal physics engine that strictly forbids self-intersection (a hard boundary clamp).

## 2. Directed Growth Vectors (L-System vs Mass-Spring Tension)
Instead of a simple "bottom-to-top" age gradient, the bark plates are driven by a sophisticated "Growth Deformation Algorithm":
- **Circumferential Tension (X-axis)**: A simulated "stress tensor" pulls the mesh laterally, mimicking the trunk expanding in girth.
- **Physics Iteration Loop**:
  1. **Initialization:** The Voronoi cells are connected by elastic springs mirroring their adjoining edges.
  2. **Stress Application:** A lateral force field (modulated by SimplexNoise to simulate uneven biological growth) stretches the nodes horizontally.
  3. **Fracture Mechanics (The Tearing Algorithm):** As tension grows, springs surpassing a specific stress threshold "snap". 
  4. **Contraction:** When a spring snaps, the dead bark plates violently pull back towards their centers of mass. The empty space created by the snapped springs forms the deep, interconnected diamond fissures.

## 3. Geometric Shapes and Complexity
Rather than drawing noisy circles, the visualization relies on the strict, tearing geometry of the polygons.
- As the iteration pushes the grid, straight Euclidean shapes deform into complex, woven diamond lattices.
- The physics engine allows for *scale-dependent tearing*: large plates might stay intact while thin strips buckle and tear apart instantly under the lateral stress.

## 4. Comprehensive Canopy Color Palette (SSoT)
The palette has been expanded to represent the entire chromatic spectrum of the *Ailanthus altissima* (Tree of Heaven), moving beyond just the gray bark into the vivid life of the tree:
- **Bark Base (Dead Plates)**: Light Gray `hsl(140, 2%, 63%)` (≈ `#9FA1A0`)
- **Deep Fissures (Shadows)**: Chestnut / Reddish-Brown `hsl(25, 60%, 30%)` (≈ `#8B4513`)
- **New Growth (Callus)**: Yellowish-Green Flowers `hsl(73, 50%, 70%)` (≈ `#C6D68F`)
- **Canopy Shadow (Accents)**: Deep Green Leaves `hsl(106, 23%, 33%)` (≈ `#4A6741`)
- **Emerging Life (Highlights)**: Bronze/Reddish Spring Leaves `hsl(26, 38%, 51%)` (≈ `#B07C55`)
